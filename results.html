Case Study: Bulgaria

Results

Project outputs for pollution, land cover change, and population exposure.

The project produced the required pollutant maps for Bulgaria for NO2, PM2.5, and PM10, including December 2023 maps, 2023 annual average maps, concentration classification maps, and 2021-2023 annual mean change maps. These outputs show the spatial pattern of air pollution in Bulgaria and identify areas where pollutant concentrations increased or decreased over time.

The land cover change result shows the main transitions in Bulgaria between 2021 and 2023. The statistical table summarizes the percentage of stable pixels, the top sources of gain, and the top destinations of loss for the assigned land cover class. This result helps explain how land cover dynamics may be related to environmental change.

The population exposure analysis produced zonal statistics, bivariate maps, and chart layers showing the relationship between pollutant concentration and population distribution in Bulgaria. The final website and WebGIS present these results through maps, tables, charts, and legends, making it possible to identify areas with both high pollution levels and high population exposure.
